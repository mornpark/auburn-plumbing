Free Johnson County Winter Emergency Guide

The 90 seconds before the plumber arrives matter most — especially in a Kansas ice storm. Here's exactly what to do. Save this page.

1. Frozen pipe → shut the main, then relieve pressure

A frozen pipe bursts when it thaws, not when it freezes. Shut your main water valve (often in the basement near the front foundation wall), then open a faucet to relieve pressure. Never thaw a pipe with an open flame.

2. Burst pipe or major leak → shut off the main

Turn the main shut-off clockwise until it stops. In many Johnson County homes the main is in the basement or utility room where the water line enters the house.

3. Overflowing toilet → shut off at the fixture

Look behind the toilet for a small oval valve on the wall. Turn clockwise. Same trick works under sinks — every fixture has its own stop valve.

4. Gas smell near the water heater → leave, then call Kansas Gas Service

Get everyone out of the house first. From a safe distance, call Kansas Gas Service at 888-482-4950, then call us. Never drain a hot tank yourself.
