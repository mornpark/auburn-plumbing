Free Homeowner's Emergency Shut-Off Guide

The 90 seconds before the plumber arrives matter most. Here's exactly what to do — save this page.

1. Burst pipe or major leak → shut off the main

Your main shut-off valve is usually at the water meter near the street, in the garage, or where the main line enters the house. Turn clockwise until it stops. In Auburn's older foothill homes, check the crawlspace entry.

2. Overflowing toilet → shut off at the fixture

Look behind the toilet for a small oval valve on the wall. Turn clockwise. Same trick works under sinks — every fixture has its own stop valve.

3. Leaking water heater → water AND power

Shut the cold-water valve on top of the tank, then kill power at the breaker (electric) or turn the gas dial to OFF. Never drain a hot tank yourself.

4. Sewage backup → stop using water entirely

Every flush and shower adds to the backup. Stop all water use, keep kids and pets away, and call us — this one's genuinely urgent.
